War Thunder organises every vehicle into tech trees, split by nation and by branch (aviation, ground, and naval). You climb each tree by spending the experience you earn in battle, working from the starting reserve vehicles up toward the modern top tier.

## The ten nations

There are ten playable nations: the USA, Germany, the USSR, Great Britain, Japan, China, Italy, France, Sweden, and Israel. Not every nation fields every branch, though. The naval trees are narrower: both the Bluewater Fleet and the Coastal Fleet list only seven nations — the USA, Germany, the USSR, Great Britain, Japan, Italy, and France. China, Sweden, and Israel have no naval trees in their current form.

<Callout type="info">A standalone Korean research tree has been mentioned by the developers as a future plan, drawing on vehicles from both South Korea and North Korea. It is **not** in the game yet, and no release date has been given.</Callout>

## Rank ceilings differ by branch

Ranks mark how far up a tree a vehicle sits, and each branch tops out at a different rank:

| Branch | Top rank |
| --- | --- |
| Aviation | Rank IX |
| Ground vehicles | Rank VIII |
| Bluewater Fleet | Rank VII |
| Coastal Fleet | Rank V |

Aviation gained Rank IX in a research-tree reshuffle: the developers announced the new top rank for the air trees in early March 2026, promoting several former top-tier aircraft into it while regrouping or relocating some Rank VIII machines. The same round of changes also began moving selected armoured vehicles within the USSR and Chinese ground trees to better line up battle ratings with ranks.

<Callout type="warning">Because reshuffles can continue patch to patch, the exact tree layout at the very top — and whether any nation's air tree differs there — is worth confirming against the current live trees before you rely on it.</Callout>

## How research works

Progress is measured in <Term id="RP">Research Points (RP)</Term>. There are two kinds. **Vehicle RP** unlocks new vehicles in a tree; **modification RP** unlocks upgrades on a vehicle you already own.

You research one vehicle at a time within a branch. While that target is selected, every vehicle of that branch you take into battle pours its RP toward it. You can also spend **Convertible RP** — a separate free pool — to speed things along; the wiki gives the rate as 1 <Term id="GE">Golden Eagle (GE)</Term> to convert 45 Convertible RP into 45 vehicle RP.

Researching a vehicle is only the first step. To actually use it, you must buy it with <Term id="SL">Silver Lions (SL)</Term> and pay to train a crew on it, also in SL. SL is the standard battle currency that covers vehicle purchases, crews, modifications, and repairs. GE is the premium currency for optional boosts and cosmetics.

## Modifications, folders, and bonuses

A vehicle's modifications are organised into tiers. Higher tiers stay locked until you have researched enough of the tier before them. There is a reward for completing a whole tier: a bonus of vehicle RP equal to 20% of that tier's total RP cost.

Vehicles can also be grouped into **folders** in the tree. When the last item of a research line is a folder, the first vehicle inside it counts as the "top" vehicle for bonus purposes.

That "top" status matters because of the daily research bonus. Owning a qualifying top vehicle of a given type, in any nation, grants a research bonus for up to five battles per day per vehicle type, resetting at 00:00 GMT. To qualify, the vehicle must be researchable (not premium, squadron, event, store, marketplace, or hidden), sit last in its tree line at the tree's maximum rank, and have a maximum <Term id="BR">BR</Term> no more than two steps below the tree's highest.

<Callout type="warning">The precise bonus percentages per tree type and rank live in a wiki table that could not be read as text, so no specific figure is quoted here. Check the current wiki before quoting numbers.</Callout>

## Boosting your earnings

Two tools speed everything up. A **Talisman** bought for a specific vehicle doubles the RP it earns (a 100% bonus) but does nothing for SL. A **Premium account** raises SL earnings by at least 50% and adds a 100% RP bonus across game modes.

<Callout type="tip">Stack a Talisman on a vehicle you enjoy and play it while Premium is active to grind a research line far faster.</Callout>

## Real-world note

The nations and vehicles modelled in War Thunder are drawn from real twentieth- and twenty-first-century military hardware, but the game's tree placement, ranks, and balance are design choices and do not mirror real-world service history or chronology.
