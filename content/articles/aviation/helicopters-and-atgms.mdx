Helicopters sit between aircraft and ground vehicles, and most of their lethality comes from guided missiles. To fly them well you need to understand how the game classifies missile guidance and how the gunner sight does the heavy lifting.

## How ATGM guidance works

War Thunder sorts <Term id="ATGM">ATGM</Term> guidance into three generations.

- **First generation, MCLOS** (Manual Command to Line of Sight): you steer the missile by hand in flight with directional controls. While you are flying the missile, the launching vehicle cannot move from its firing spot.
- **Second generation, <Term id="SACLOS">SACLOS</Term>** (Semi-Automatic Command to Line of Sight): you only hold the crosshair on the target, and the game automatically corrects the missile toward where you are aiming. You must keep the reticle on the target until impact, which leaves you exposed for the whole flight.
- **Third generation, fire-and-forget**: a laser or IR seeker acquires the target so you can leave the firing position the instant the missile launches. The wiki cites the FGM-148 Javelin as an example.

A useful trick with SACLOS: you can override the automatic correction by aiming at a point the missile has already flown past, which hands you manual control of the crosshair-guided flight.

Warheads matter too. Tandem-charge missiles carry two HEAT charges, the first to defeat reactive armour sitting above the base plate and the second to punch through what is left. Some top-attack missiles launch upward and dive onto the thin roof armour of a target.

<Callout type="warning">
The terms "beam-riding" and "laser beam-riding" do not appear by name on the Tier-1 ATGM mechanics page, which only describes MCLOS, SACLOS, and fire-and-forget. Whether laser guidance is modeled as a category distinct from fire-and-forget IR is also not stated outright. Treat any beam-riding or separate laser-guidance claim as unverified against the current patch.
</Callout>

## Targeting from the cockpit

For helicopters, every air-to-ground missile except the self-guiding ones is steered with the cursor, just like aircraft SACLOS weapons. Most helicopters give you a dedicated gunner view, and the missile is directed from there independently of the airframe.

That gunner sight is the key tool. Its stabilization compensates for the helicopter's own motion and holds the image steady, so you can guide a missile smoothly even while throwing the aircraft around. More advanced helicopters can lock the stabilized sight onto a moving target for semi-autonomous guidance. The view also shows the maximum guidance distance and a live range readout to whatever point you are aiming at. Attack drones, when present, use the same guidance and feedback approach.

### Case study: the Mi-24P

The Mi-24P guide describes its 9M114 Shturm as SACLOS-guided: you manually guide the missile onto the target with mouse or joystick. The guide also warns that the Shturm grows less responsive and harder to control at long range, so feed it small, smooth corrections rather than sharp inputs.

<Callout type="warning">
Numeric Shturm stats (range, velocity, penetration) seen in the vehicle guide text are guide-stated and subject to per-patch rebalancing. Check the live stat card before treating any figure as current under Update Heavy Cavalry (2.57).
</Callout>

## Where you actually fly

War Thunder offers cooperative PvE involving helicopters: Assault Mode pits aircraft, ground vehicles, and helicopters against AI, and there are dedicated Helicopter Battles.

Enduring Confrontation (EC) is the larger, free-form mode, and a simplified Arcade version of it is the only separate dedicated game mode for helicopters. EC battles run as Events, reached from the "Events and Tournaments" section under the "To Battle" button. You can queue normally or pick from a list of active rooms sorted by country and rank. EC gives you unlimited respawns subject to certain rules, with victory points awarded for explicit and hidden objectives, so the momentum of a match can swing back and forth repeatedly.

<Callout type="tip">
Because EC lets you respawn freely, treat early sorties as reconnaissance. Spend your first runs learning where AA clusters before you commit to a long SACLOS engagement.
</Callout>

## Real-world note

The FGM-148 Javelin is a real shoulder-fired anti-tank missile known for its lock-on-before-launch, fire-and-forget seeker and top-attack profile. Reactive armour and tandem-charge warheads are likewise genuine arms-race technologies: tandem warheads exist specifically to defeat the explosive reactive armour fitted to many modern tanks.
