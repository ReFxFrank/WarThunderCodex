War Thunder's economy has two interlocking halves. One is **currency**: the Silver Lions you earn, the Golden Eagles you buy, and the Warbonds you accumulate through the Battle Pass. The other is **progression**: several distinct flavors of Research Points (<Term id="RP">RP</Term>) that unlock vehicles, modifications, and crew abilities. Understanding which currency feeds which system is the key to spending efficiently.

## Currencies

**Silver Lions (SL)** are the base earnable currency, paid out after every battle. The payout scales with the game mode, your in-battle performance and activity, and whether you won or lost. SL covers nearly all routine costs: buying researched vehicles, training crews, purchasing researched modifications, post-battle repairs, replenishing ammunition and weaponry, in-battle crew replacements, and unlocking the earlier extra crew slots.

**Golden Eagles (GE)** are the premium currency. You buy them in bundles from the Gaijin Store; they are only occasionally awarded (for example, to top tournament finishers) and generally cannot be earned through ordinary play. GE buys premium vehicles, premium account time, research and crew-training acceleration, instant modification unlocks, the advanced crew slots beyond what SL covers, cosmetics, and the conversion of Convertible RP into usable vehicle RP.

## Research Points

RP is not a single pool. Different types serve different ends:

- **Vehicle RP** unlocks new vehicles in a tree.
- **Modification RP** unlocks a specific vehicle's upgrades.
- **Convertible RP** is a free pool applicable to any nation's research.
- **Crew points** improve crew skills.
- **Squadron RP** researches squadron vehicles.

Like SL, RP is earned in battle and scales with mode, performance, and outcome, with a "skill bonus" multiplier that can boost RP for destroying enemies or hitting damage thresholds. Convertible RP can be turned into vehicle research using GE at a rate of **45 RP per 1 GE**.

<Callout type="info">Researching a modification only makes it available. Each one must still be **purchased with SL** before you can equip it. A vehicle's per-modification RP and SL costs appear in its "Modifications and economy" section on the wiki.</Callout>

## Premium account and premium vehicles

These are different things. A **premium vehicle** is a single vehicle you own. A **premium account** is a time-limited membership that boosts earnings across your whole lineup. You can buy premium time with GE in-game or with real money on the Gaijin Store.

The membership raises both RP and SL earnings, and adds expense compensation that kicks in when automatic post-battle repair, ammo purchases, and crew replenishment would otherwise leave you net-negative in SL. It also bundles conveniences like extra decal slots, the ability to ban maps from your rotation per game mode, and hiding your nickname in battle.

<Callout type="warning">The wiki cites figures such as +100% RP and roughly +50% to +100% SL for premium account in Random Battles, plus a Warbond balance cap of 3,600. These numbers are rebalanced frequently and the source articles may carry older revisions, so verify them against the current patch before relying on exact values.</Callout>

## Crew skills and qualifications

Crew skills are grouped by vehicle type. Aviation crews cover pilot skills, defensive gunners, and logistical services; ground crews cover driver, gunner, commander, loader, and radio operator; naval crews cover commander, observers, engine room, gunners, and damage control.

Each crew advances through qualification tiers:

| Tier | How obtained |
|------|--------------|
| **Basic** | Spending earned crew points |
| **Expert** | Purchased with SL |
| **Ace** | Typically earned, or bought with GE |

For aircraft, Expert and Ace qualifications stack on top of your invested skill levels, and reaching full effectiveness requires maxing both the skills and both qualifications. For ground and naval crews, the invested skill levels supply only part of the crew's potential, with the Expert and Ace qualifications plus the commander's **Leadership** skill supplying the rest. Leadership is worth singling out: it lifts every other crew member's skills, which makes it both powerful and expensive to train. Crew points come from battle participation and can be accelerated with GE.

## Warbonds and the Battle Pass

**Warbonds (WB)** are earned by progressing through Battle Pass levels and completing tasks. They do not expire and carry across seasons, though your balance is capped. You spend them in the Warbond Shop, which unlocks in six levels tied to Battle Pass milestones and rotates its stock of camouflages, talismans, premium vehicles, boosters, and cosmetics each season.

The Battle Pass itself has a free track and a paid track. The standard paid pass is bought with GE; an improved option can open a batch of levels immediately. Each level needs 10 progress points, earned from daily logins, battle tasks, and season challenges. Rewards span SL, boosters, Warbonds, decorations, profile icons, and seasonal premium vehicles. A season runs a minimum of about 13 weeks, and to take part you must own at least one researchable Rank II (or higher) vehicle.
