New players agonize over which nation to pick first, as if the choice locks them in forever. It does not. The mechanics that actually govern your progress, currencies and research, work the same way across every tree, so the smarter question is how to earn efficiently from your very first reserve match.

## Nation choice is reversible

Pick the line whose vehicles you find fun to look at and drive. That sounds flippant, but it is the honest advice: you will play hundreds of battles, and enjoyment keeps you playing, which is what actually drives progress.

The deeper reason choice is not a trap is <Term id="RP">convertible RP</Term>. Alongside the <Term id="RP">RP</Term> that unlocks a specific vehicle, every battle also fills a general "convertible" pool. By spending <Term id="GE">Golden Eagles</Term>, you can apply that pool to *any* nation's research (the wiki lists a fixed rate of 1 GE per 45 convertible RP). So even if you switch trees later, the experience you banked is not wasted.

<Callout type="warning">
Community guides often crown one nation as "easiest for beginners." Treat that as opinion. No Tier-1 source ranks nations for new players, and the claim should be verified against the current patch's balance rather than taken as fact.
</Callout>

## How the economy works

War Thunder runs on three spendable currencies. **Silver Lions** (<Term id="SL">SL</Term>) are the everyday earnable currency, spent on buying vehicles, training crews, installing modifications, repairs, and resupply. **Golden Eagles** (GE) are premium currency, bought with real money rather than earned through normal play. **Warbonds** are earned by reaching specific Battle Pass levels and spent in a rotating Warbond shop. (Research itself is tracked separately as RP, not a currency.)

Both SL and RP scale with game mode, your in-battle activity, vehicles destroyed, and whether you won. A "Skill Bonus" rewards opponents defeated and damage dealt (up to +100% RP), so aggressive, useful play pays better than passive survival.

## Research efficiently

RP comes in distinct flavors: vehicle RP unlocks new vehicles, modification RP (each vehicle accrues its own pool) unlocks that vehicle's upgrades, and the convertible pool described above.

The tech tree is gated. Vehicles connect by arrows; you must research and then purchase each one before the next opens, with rank criteria layered on top. Crucially, *where* you research from matters:

| Situation | Effect on vehicle RP |
| --- | --- |
| Researching far below the target's rank | Penalty (the wiki cites up to -95% at large rank gaps) |
| Researching the directly connected next unlock | Bonus (the wiki cites 130% in Arcade, 110% in Realistic and Simulator) |

The takeaway: grind toward your next vehicle using something close to it in the tree, not a low-rank favorite you've outgrown.

## Free bonuses, used daily

The **Vehicle Research Bonus** is the single best habit for new players, and it costs nothing. Each day, up to 5 battles per vehicle type per nation earn bonus RP, resetting at 00:00 GMT. The bonus is computed from the base RP you earned and scales with the researched vehicle's rank (the devblog cites roughly 25% at the low ranks up to 100% at the top).

There is a catch: the bonus unlocks once you own a researchable (non-premium, non-squadron, non-event, non-marketplace) "top" vehicle of a given type, last in its line at the tree's maximum rank and within two Battle Rating steps of the tree's highest. Owning a top aircraft, for example, enables the aircraft bonus across *all* nations, but not ground vehicles. Types are tracked separately: Army, Helicopters, Aviation, Bluewater fleet, and Coastal fleet.

## Common mistakes

- **Rushing your <Term id="BR">BR</Term>.** Climbing faster than your skill (and your crews and modifications) leaves you outmatched and broke on repairs.
- **Ignoring the daily bonus.** Those 5 battles per type are free RP you are leaving on the table.
- **Spreading too thin.** Focus one line at a time so your research lands on directly-connected vehicles.

## When premium helps

A **Premium account** grants +100% RP in all modes plus Silver Lion bonuses, and it compensates you when automatic repair, ammo, and crew replenishment would otherwise leave a battle net-negative on SL. A vehicle **talisman** doubles RP for that one vehicle (but not SL). **Premium vehicles** carry a higher built-in RP and SL multiplier than regular vehicles. These stack conceptually.

The **Battle Pass** is a seasonal track (each season runs at least 13 weeks) needing only a Rank II vehicle to join, with a large free reward portion: SL, boosters, Warbonds, and more, earned through daily logins, Battle Tasks, and Season Challenges. A paid "Improved" upgrade unlocks a block of season levels at once.

<Callout type="tip">
None of this is required to progress. Premium speeds the grind; it does not gate it. Start with the free daily bonus and a single focused tree before spending anything.
</Callout>

<Callout type="warning">
Specific repair costs, vehicle prices, crew-training costs, and GE prices for the Battle Pass are not quoted here. They are per-vehicle, change every patch, and should be checked in-game against the current version.
</Callout>

## Real-world note

The nations represented, the United States, Germany, the USSR, Britain, Japan, and others, fielded these vehicles across the mid-20th century. In-game balance groups them by performance, not by history, so a vehicle's match-up reflects game mechanics rather than wartime reality.
